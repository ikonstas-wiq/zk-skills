- **To peers:** <balanced technical/strategic, collaborative>

- **To external:** <professional warmth, clear value>

## Third-party organisation naming

- Do not name a third-party organisation unless the source clearly intends it named for that audience.
- Generalise to a truthful role/category when identity is unnecessary (`a global recruiter`, not a company name).
- Keep the name when it is public, approved, required for provenance, or needed to understand the content.
- Never invent an organisation, relationship, approval, or attribution.

## Meeting and transcript sensitivity

Treat transcript-derived content as confidential in shareable outputs. Facts,
decisions, positions, and themes may inform the result, but render them without
identifying or incriminating attendees.

1. No verbatim quotes attributed to a named person. Paraphrase the position neutrally.
2. No attribution of opinions, scepticism, frustration, conflict, or political stance to a named person. Use neutral framing ("concerns were raised", "the team debated").
3. Remove side-channel commentary — DMs, post-meeting reflections, off-the-record remarks, personal asides.
4. No personnel, HR, leave, health, or other sensitive personal details. If operationally relevant, describe capacity or timing without naming the circumstance.
5. Share decisions and outcomes, not the interpersonal path that produced them.
6. When a stakeholder's identity is not necessary, abstract one level ("stakeholder feedback indicated").

Test: if the output were forwarded to an attendee, would they recognise
themselves characterised in a way they did not approve? If yes, rewrite.

For explicitly personal, non-shareable notes these rules may relax only after
confirming the output stays private. Factual-integrity and no-fabrication rules
always remain in force.

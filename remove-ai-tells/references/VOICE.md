# Positive Voice — What the prose should read like

`TELLS.md` lists what to remove. This file states what to aim for. The De-Slop Pass removes tells; this is the target the rewrite steers toward. Adapt the specifics to your own house style — the principles hold regardless.

## Principles

- **Plain language over polish.** Use the word you'd say to a colleague, not the word that sounds impressive. If a simpler word carries the meaning, use it.
- **Own your position.** State the claim the author holds. Drop the reflexive hedges ("may", "often", "generally"); if a caveat matters, state it once, neutrally, and move on.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Anchor every abstract claim to one particular — a number, a name, a specific case. "Ten minutes instead of three hours" beats "significantly faster".
- **Active voice, real subjects.** Name who does what. "The security leads pushed back" beats "concerns were raised".
- **Vary the rhythm.** Mix short sentences against longer ones. Let the key line stand alone. Uniform sentence length is the surest sign a machine wrote it.
- **Let ideas end.** No bow-tie closers, no "in conclusion", no summarising what you just said. Stop when the point is made.
- **Write like you speak.** If a sentence sounds like a slide deck rather than something said to a person, rewrite it.

## Punctuation house style (configure to taste)

The catalogue treats punctuation by density, not presence. Layer your own house rules on top. A common short-form convention:

- **Em dashes:** fine in long-form at low density; in email and chat, prefer colons or parentheses.
- **Exclamation marks:** default to none.
- **Emoji:** none in formal docs, reports, or HTML; sparing in chat only.
- **Bold:** structural (subheadings) yes; emphasis-on-key-lines no — let the words land.

## The clarity standard (Paul Graham)

The banned lists in `TELLS.md` are symptoms, not the disease. The real test: re-read each line; if it's even slightly unclear, ask "what did I actually mean to say?" and rewrite until it's clear. A sentence that survives only because it *sounds* polished still fails.
